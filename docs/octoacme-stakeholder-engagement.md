# OctoAcme — Stakeholder Engagement Checklist

## Purpose
Provide a systematic approach to identifying, analyzing, and engaging stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle to ensure alignment, support, and successful outcomes.

## What is a Stakeholder?
A stakeholder is any individual or group who:
- Has interest in or is impacted by the project
- Can influence the project's success or failure
- Provides resources, approvals, or input
- Will use or benefit from the project's deliverables

---

## Stakeholder Identification Checklist

### Initial Stakeholder Mapping
- [ ] Identify executive sponsors and decision-makers
- [ ] List primary users and customers
- [ ] Identify funding sources and budget approvers
- [ ] Map impacted teams and departments
- [ ] Include compliance and regulatory stakeholders
- [ ] Identify subject matter experts and advisors
- [ ] Consider vendors or external partners
- [ ] Document secondary stakeholders (indirectly impacted)

### Stakeholder Analysis Matrix
Create a matrix with the following information for each stakeholder:

| Name/Group | Role | Interest Level | Influence Level | Communication Needs | Key Concerns |
|------------|------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| [Name] | [Title] | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | [Frequency/Format] | [Concerns] |

### Power-Interest Grid
Categorize stakeholders into four quadrants:

**High Power, High Interest** (Manage Closely)
- [ ] Identify key decision-makers and champions
- [ ] Plan frequent, detailed engagement
- [ ] Address concerns proactively

**High Power, Low Interest** (Keep Satisfied)
- [ ] Identify executives and approvers
- [ ] Provide high-level status updates
- [ ] Escalate only critical issues

**Low Power, High Interest** (Keep Informed)
- [ ] Identify team members and end users
- [ ] Share regular updates and progress
- [ ] Gather detailed feedback

**Low Power, Low Interest** (Monitor)
- [ ] Identify peripheral stakeholders
- [ ] Provide periodic general updates
- [ ] Monitor for changes in interest or influence

## Stakeholder Engagement Planning

### Communication Strategy
- [ ] Define communication objectives for each stakeholder group
- [ ] Determine preferred communication channels (email, meetings, Slack, etc.)
- [ ] Set frequency of updates (daily, weekly, monthly, milestone-based)
- [ ] Assign responsibility for stakeholder communication
- [ ] Create templates for consistent messaging
- [ ] Plan for two-way communication (feedback loops)

### Engagement Schedule
- [ ] Schedule project kickoff with all key stakeholders
- [ ] Plan regular status updates (weekly/monthly)
- [ ] Schedule milestone reviews and demos
- [ ] Set up office hours or Q&A sessions
- [ ] Plan for ad-hoc communication needs
- [ ] Schedule post-project review and feedback session

### Messaging and Positioning
- [ ] Develop clear value proposition for each stakeholder group
- [ ] Prepare elevator pitch (30-second summary)
- [ ] Create talking points for common questions
- [ ] Anticipate objections and prepare responses
- [ ] Align messaging with organizational priorities
- [ ] Tailor technical depth to audience

---

## Ongoing Stakeholder Management

### Regular Engagement Activities
- [ ] Send weekly/monthly status reports
- [ ] Host sprint demos or milestone reviews
- [ ] Conduct stakeholder surveys or feedback sessions
- [ ] Share project dashboards and metrics
- [ ] Provide early previews of key deliverables
- [ ] Celebrate successes and milestones publicly

### Addressing Stakeholder Concerns
- [ ] Listen actively and acknowledge concerns
- [ ] Document concerns in decision log or risk register
- [ ] Provide timely, transparent responses
- [ ] Involve stakeholders in problem-solving when appropriate
- [ ] Escalate issues that require higher-level decisions
- [ ] Follow up to confirm concerns are addressed

### Managing Changes
- [ ] Communicate scope changes clearly and promptly
- [ ] Explain impact on timeline, budget, and resources
- [ ] Obtain approval for significant changes
- [ ] Update project documentation to reflect changes
- [ ] Re-engage stakeholders when priorities shift
- [ ] Document change decisions and rationale

## Stakeholder Communication Templates

### Project Kickoff Announcement
```
Subject: [Project Name] — Kickoff and Overview

Dear Stakeholders,

We're excited to announce the launch of [Project Name]. This email provides an overview of the project and how we'll keep you informed.

**Project Overview:**
- Purpose: [Brief description]
- Goals: [Key objectives]
- Timeline: [Start date] to [End date]
- Team: [Key team members]

**What This Means for You:**
- [Specific impact or benefit for this stakeholder group]

**How We'll Communicate:**
- [Update frequency and format]

**How You Can Help:**
- [Specific asks or ways to engage]

**Next Steps:**
- [Upcoming milestones or meetings]

Questions? Contact: [PM Name, Email]

Thank you for your support!
```

### Monthly Stakeholder Update
```
Subject: [Project Name] — [Month] Update

## Executive Summary
[2-3 sentence overview of status and key developments]

## Accomplishments This Month
- [Major milestone or achievement]
- [Key deliverable completed]
- [Important decision made]

## What's Next
- [Upcoming milestone]
- [Planned activities]
- [Expected deliverables]

## Metrics & Progress
- [Key metric]: [Value and trend]
- [Timeline status]: On track / At risk / Delayed
- [Budget status]: Within budget / [Variance]

## Risks & Issues
- [High-priority risk or issue]
- Mitigation: [Action being taken]

## Decisions Needed
- [Decision required]: [By when]
- [Approval needed]: [Details]

## How You Can Help
- [Specific request or ask]

**Detailed Dashboard:** [Link to project board or metrics]

Questions? Contact: [PM Name, Email]
```

### Urgent Escalation Communication
```
Subject: URGENT: [Project Name] — [Issue Summary]

Dear [Stakeholder Name],

We need your immediate attention on a critical issue affecting [Project Name].

**Issue:** [Brief description]

**Impact:**
- [Who/what is affected]
- [Severity and scope]

**Action Needed:**
- [Specific decision or approval required]
- [Deadline]: [Date/Time]

**Context:**
- [Background information]
- [Why this escalation is necessary]

**Options Considered:**
1. [Option 1]: [Pros/Cons]
2. [Option 2]: [Pros/Cons]

**Recommendation:** [Proposed solution]

**Next Steps:**
- Please respond by [Date/Time]
- [Meeting scheduled or requested]

Contact: [PM Name, Phone, Email]

Thank you for your urgent attention to this matter.
```

## Change Management for Stakeholders

### Preparing Stakeholders for Change
- [ ] Identify how each stakeholder will be impacted
- [ ] Communicate the "why" behind the change
- [ ] Provide training or resources as needed
- [ ] Address resistance and concerns empathetically
- [ ] Create champions and change advocates
- [ ] Plan for transition period support

### User Adoption Strategy
- [ ] Develop user onboarding materials
- [ ] Create self-service documentation and FAQs
- [ ] Offer training sessions or workshops
- [ ] Provide hands-on demos and walkthroughs
- [ ] Set up support channels for questions
- [ ] Measure adoption metrics and adjust strategy

## Stakeholder Feedback Loop

### Gathering Feedback
- [ ] Conduct stakeholder satisfaction surveys
- [ ] Hold retrospective sessions with key stakeholders
- [ ] Collect feedback during demos and reviews
- [ ] Monitor usage metrics and behavior data
- [ ] Schedule 1:1 interviews for deep insights
- [ ] Create feedback channels (surveys, forms, office hours)

### Acting on Feedback
- [ ] Analyze feedback for patterns and themes
- [ ] Prioritize feedback based on impact and feasibility
- [ ] Communicate what will be addressed and when
- [ ] Explain decisions for feedback not being acted upon
- [ ] Close the loop by sharing outcomes of changes made
- [ ] Thank stakeholders for their input

## Difficult Stakeholder Scenarios

### Managing Conflicting Priorities
- **Situation:** Multiple stakeholders have competing demands  
- **Strategy:**
  - Facilitate discussion to understand each perspective
  - Use data and success metrics to guide prioritization
  - Escalate to executive sponsor if consensus can't be reached
  - Document decision rationale transparently

### Dealing with Disengaged Stakeholders
- **Situation:** Stakeholder is unresponsive or uninvolved  
- **Strategy:**
  - Understand root cause (too busy, unclear value, etc.)
  - Adjust communication format or frequency
  - Highlight specific value or impact for them
  - Escalate if their input is critical for project success

### Handling Scope Creep from Stakeholders
- **Situation:** Stakeholder continuously requests additions  
- **Strategy:**
  - Use formal change request process
  - Explain impact on timeline, budget, and resources
  - Offer to backlog requests for future phases
  - Reinforce original scope and success criteria
  - Involve executive sponsor if needed

### Addressing Negative Stakeholders
- **Situation:** Stakeholder opposes project or spreads negativity  
- **Strategy:**
  - Listen to understand their concerns
  - Address legitimate issues proactively
  - Involve them in problem-solving
  - Build trust through transparency and small wins
  - Escalate if behavior is obstructive

## Success Metrics for Stakeholder Engagement

### Quantitative Metrics
- Stakeholder satisfaction score (surveys)
- Response rate to stakeholder communications
- Attendance at stakeholder meetings
- Number of escalations or conflicts
- Time to resolve stakeholder issues
- Adoption rate of deliverables

### Qualitative Indicators
- Stakeholder feedback sentiment
- Level of active participation in project activities
- Quality of feedback and input provided
- Willingness to advocate for the project
- Alignment on project goals and priorities

## Tips for Effective Stakeholder Engagement

1. **Start early** — Engage stakeholders from project inception
2. **Be proactive** — Communicate before being asked
3. **Listen actively** — Seek to understand before being understood
4. **Be transparent** — Share both successes and challenges
5. **Manage expectations** — Be realistic about what's achievable
6. **Follow through** — Deliver on commitments and promises
7. **Show value** — Demonstrate tangible benefits regularly
8. **Build relationships** — Invest time in personal connections
9. **Adapt your approach** — Tailor engagement to each stakeholder
10. **Document everything** — Keep records of decisions and feedback
